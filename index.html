Attention Experiment — Two Stages

Can you trust
your own attention?

This experiment has two stages. In each you will be asked to watch carefully — but what "watching carefully" means will be very different. Together they reveal something uncomfortable about what human oversight actually is.

Stage 1 — Track a ball under three identical cups  /  Stage 2 — Watch a security camera feed

STAGE 1 OF 2
The ball and cups

Your task

A ball will be placed under one of three identical cups. The cups will then shuffle. Your job is to keep track of which cup is hiding the ball — and click it when the shuffle stops.

That is all you need to do. Focus entirely on the ball.

The room around you will also be visible. You do not need to pay attention to it. Just track the ball.

STAGE 1 OF 2
The ball and cups

Track the ball

The ball is under one cup. Study the room. Click Ready when you want the shuffle to begin.

    What this shows

    Focused attention is a spotlight — it illuminates one thing and darkens everything else. This is not carelessness. It is how perception is structured. Now we remove the spotlight entirely and see what happens.

      The finding

      Think about what this means for human oversight of automated systems. If you miss changes when focused on one thing, and also miss them when watching everything — when exactly is attention a reliable safeguard?
